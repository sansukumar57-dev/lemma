05 — Design Rationale
Marks
Why these symbols

Every direction borrows from the visual language of mathematics — not because Lemma is a math product, but because math has developed the richest vocabulary for the idea of proven, foundational units.

The ∴ symbol means "therefore" — the hinge that connects premises to conclusions. Three dots that carry the whole weight of logical consequence. It's immediately distinct and almost never used in software.

The ascending bars are the most intuitive: steps building toward something. The QED square □ is for those who know what it means — quietly confident.

Loading
Why sequential, not spinning

A spinner communicates nothing except "wait." It implies the system is working but doesn't say anything about the nature of the work.

Sequential, step-based animation implies that something is being built — premises accumulating toward a conclusion. It's patient rather than anxious. It fits who Lemma is for.

The recommendation is Direction A (proof steps) for most loading contexts, and the wordmark build for the full splash screen. The ∴ animation works beautifully for inline loading states — small, meaningful, unmistakably Lemma.
